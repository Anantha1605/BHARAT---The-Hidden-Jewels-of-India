A Glimpse into History
Orchha, founded in the 16th century by Rudra Pratap Singh, flourished under Bundela rulers, becoming a cultural bastion with grand palaces, forts, and temples along the Betwa River.

Architectural Splendour
Orchha's architectural landscape blends Rajput and Mughal styles, featuring the Jahangir Mahal's intricate designs, the Raja Mahal's exquisite murals, and the awe-inspiring Chaturbhuj Temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Cultural and Spiritual Significance
Orchha's spiritual significance shines through the Ram Raja Temple, attracting pilgrims. Vibrant festivals like Ram Navami and Dussehra celebrate devotion, righteousness, and the victory of good, enveloping the town in divine energy.
