A Glimpse into History
Unakoti, dating back to the 7th-9th centuries AD, features rock-cut carvings. Legend says Lord Shiva turned 99,99,999 gods to stone for not awakening, creating this divine spectacle.

Architectural Splendour
Unakoti's architecture features colossal rock carvings up to 30 feet high, including Lord Shiva's "Unakotiswara Kal Bhairava," Ganesha, Durga, and Vishnu, showcasing extraordinary artistic skill and spiritual devotion.

Cultural and Spiritual Significance
Unakoti, a major Shaiva pilgrimage site, showcases ancient rock-cut sculptures. The Ashokashtami Mela and Shiva Chaturdashi festivals draw thousands, blending devotion with cultural performances, rituals, and community bonding.
