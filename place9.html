A Glimpse into History
Patan, founded in 746 AD by Vanraj Chavda, was the Chalukya dynasty's capital from the 8th to 13th century, flourishing as a cultural and trade hub with rich Jain and Hindu heritage.

Architectural Splendour
Patan's architectural marvels include Rani ki Vav, a UNESCO site with intricate carvings, and Sahastralinga Talav, an ancient reservoir symbolizing devotion to Lord Shiva with numerous shivalingas.

Cultural and Spiritual Significance
