CHITKUL
Himachal Pradesh
Nestled in Kinnaur, Himachal Pradesh, Chitkul is a serene village at 3,450 meters, the last Indian village before the Indo-Tibetan border, offering stunning natural beauty and spiritual tranquility.

A Glimpse into History
Chitkul, an ancient trade route between India and Tibet, enriched by cultural exchanges, is steeped in history and mythology, and mentioned in ancient Hindu texts, adding to its captivating allure.

Architectural Splendour
Chitkul's traditional wooden houses with carved facades and slate roofs, built for harsh Himalayan winters, highlight a deep environmental connection. The Mathi Temple, dedicated to Goddess Mathi, exemplifies exquisite regional architecture.

Cultural and Spiritual Significance
Chitkul is a spiritual haven with the Mathi Temple, where Goddess Mathi is revered. Festivals like Fulaich celebrate her, filling the village with music, dance, and communal harmony.
