A Glimpse into History
Yuksom, established in 1642 AD as Sikkim's first capital, witnessed the coronation of its first Chogyal, Phuntsog Namgyal, uniting the state under Buddhist order and drawing pilgrims and history enthusiasts.

Architectural Splendour
Yuksom boasts architectural marvels like the Norbugang Chorten and Dubdi Monastery, showcasing exquisite murals, thangkas, and statues, reflecting the region's rich spiritual and artistic heritage.

Cultural and Spiritual Significance
Yuksom, rich in Buddhist heritage, is a spiritual hub with vibrant festivals like Losar and Saga Dawa, celebrating Tibetan New Year and Buddha's life through traditional dances, music, and prayers.
