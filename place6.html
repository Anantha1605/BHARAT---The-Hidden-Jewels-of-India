A Glimpse into History
Kapurthala, founded in the 11th century by Rana Kapur, flourished under the Ahluwalia dynasty, particularly Maharaja Jagatjit Singh, who modernized the town, earning it the title "Paris of Punjab."

Architectural Splendour
Kapurthala showcases architectural wonders like Jagatjit Palace, Moorish Mosque, and Elysee Palace, blending Indo-French styles and rich cultural heritage, reflecting the town's royal history and cosmopolitan charm.

Cultural and Spiritual Significance
Kapurthala blends spirituality and culture, with Kanjli Mata Temple and serene Shalimar Gardens. Festivals like Hola Mohalla and Baisakhi highlight its vibrant traditions, attracting devotees and visitors alike.
